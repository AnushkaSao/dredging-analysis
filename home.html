What is Dredging?

Dredging is the process of excavating and removing sediments, debris, or other materials from the bottom of water bodies, such as rivers, lakes, harbors, or coastal areas. The primary purpose of dredging is to create or maintain navigable waterways, ports, and harbors, as well as to address various environmental and engineering needs.
